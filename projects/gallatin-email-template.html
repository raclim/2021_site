About

As a digital accessibility assistant for NYU Gallatin's Educational Technology team, I was tasked with redesigning accessible e-mail templates for This Week at Gallatin and Gallatin in the News in accordance with WGAC 2.0 Standards and NYU Gallatin's Style Guide.

This was done in ConstantContact.

ReDesign Objectives

Accessibility

Snippets of previous e-mails for This Week at Gallatin and Gallatin in the News shown below.

After studying the previous templates for these e-mails, I determined these aspects of the WGAC 2.0 Accessibility Standards were relevant to the redesign:

  • Hierarchal headings
  • Color Contrast
  • Information not represented solely by color
  • Unambigious phrasing of links

I also aimed to improve readability, as the content was text-heavy combined with small font.

Conformity with NYU Identity

In addition to the outlined fonts and colors schemes, the NYU Style Guide also emphasized maintaining a visual identity revolving around modernity, activeness, and intelligence.

ReDesign

For both e-mails, I sifted through previously sent campaigns respectively to determine which headers and content appeared the most to recreate structure.

With Gallatin's color sheme (blue, red, grey, dark grey, white, and black), I utilized large, flat color blocks and bold sans-serifed font to emanate sharpness, liveliness, and a sense of pop. I also restricted the usage of red to text, as it is a difficult color to pair correctly for color contrast and large amounts of it combined with the vibrant blue were straining on the eyes.

I also created headers for each e-mail. These headers were created after the e-mail templates were completed to ensure a unified appearance.

Redesign: This Week at Gallatin

This Week at Gallatin informs viewers about the weekly events at Gallatin. Presented similarly to a powerpoint, the e-mail has slides representing a day of the week, which display the event image, information, and RSVP or link button.

Following the slide format, I placed the same content in a large rectangle but decreased the image size and increased text size. I implemented these sizes because it'd pressure the person filling in the template to decrease the amount of text to maintain a visual balance between image and text.

I also had each day of the week delineated by a full-width band of color, in case anyone wanted to place information relevant to that day outside of the slide format. The colors (blue and light grey) of each band and slide alternate by day.

Redesign: Gallatin in the News

Gallatin in the News is an update of notable events involving Gallatin students, alumni, and faculty. Involving much more content than This Week at Gallatin, I decided to prioritize simplicity, consistency, and uniformity, as the previous e-mails did not have a consistent format for organizing images or content.

I placed these visual markers for each aspect of the e-mail:

  • Blue lines to dilineate major categories
  • Light Grey boxes to outline images
  • Red headings also differentiated by capitalized letters

For the images, I also included three different ways the images could be implemented: alone, paired, or as a set of three.
